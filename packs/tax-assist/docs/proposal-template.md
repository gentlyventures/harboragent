## 2. Individual Tax Changes

### 2.1 Inflation Adjustments

**Tax Brackets and Standard Deduction**

The IRS has adjusted 2025 tax brackets and standard deduction amounts for inflation:

- **Standard Deduction:**
  - Single: $14,600 (up from $14,200)
  - Married Filing Jointly: $29,200 (up from $28,400)
  - Head of Household: $21,900 (up from $21,300)

**Impact:** Most taxpayers will see a slight increase in standard deduction, potentially reducing taxable income.

### 2.2 Retirement Contribution Limits

**2025 Contribution Limits (Increased)**

- **401(k) Elective Deferral:** $23,000 (up from $22,500)
- **IRA Contribution:** $7,000 (up from $6,500)
- **IRA Catch-up (50+):** $1,000 (unchanged)
- **SIMPLE IRA:** $16,000 (up from $15,500)

**Action Items:**

- Review your retirement contribution strategy for 2025

- Consider maximizing contributions before year-end

- Verify employer matching contributions

### 2.3 Standard Mileage Rates

**2025 Mileage Rates**

- **Business:** 67 cents per mile (up from 65.5 cents)
- **Medical/Moving:** 21 cents per mile
- **Charitable:** 14 cents per mile

**Impact:** If you use the standard mileage rate for business expenses, you'll see a slight increase in deductions.

## 3. Digital Assets and Cryptocurrency

### 3.1 New Form 1099-DA (Digital Asset Broker Reporting)

**What's New:**

Starting in 2025, digital asset brokers (exchanges, platforms) are required to issue **Form 1099-DA** for cryptocurrency and digital asset transactions.

**What This Means:**

- You may receive a new form (1099-DA) from cryptocurrency exchanges

- This form will include transaction details, cost basis, and proceeds

- You'll need to report these transactions on your tax return

**Action Items:**

- Gather all cryptocurrency transaction records

- Report any digital asset exchanges or sales

- Provide us with Form 1099-DA if you receive one

- Be prepared to answer questions about your crypto activity

### 3.2 Virtual Currency Reporting

**Updated Requirements:**

The IRS has clarified reporting requirements for virtual currency transactions, including:

- Capital gains/losses reporting on Form 8949

- Cost basis tracking requirements

- Wash sale rules for digital assets

**Impact:** More comprehensive reporting of cryptocurrency transactions is now required.

## 4. Pass-Through Entities (If Applicable)

### 4.1 Schedule K-1 Updates

**Partnership and S-Corporation Returns**

If you receive income from partnerships or S-corporations (Schedule K-1), there are updates for 2025:

- New data elements and disclosure requirements

- Updated allocation and distribution reporting

- State pass-through entity (PTE) tax treatment clarifications

**Action Items:**

- Ensure you receive all K-1s by the required deadline (typically March 15)

- Review K-1 information for accuracy

- Provide us with K-1s as soon as you receive them

### 4.2 Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction

**Forms 8995 and 8995-A Updates**

The QBI deduction calculation has been updated for 2025:

- Updated computation rules

- Threshold and phase-out adjustments

- K-1 income allocation requirements

**Impact:** If you have pass-through business income, the QBI deduction calculation may change.
